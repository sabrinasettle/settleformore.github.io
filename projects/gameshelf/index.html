GameShelf

July 2023 - September 2023

Overview

About GameShelf

GameShelf is a social cataloguing mobile app and responsive website for sharing, discovering, and connecting over video games.

Problem

How might we enhance the gaming community's experience by offering a seamless user-friendly mobile app that allows gamers to instantly catalog, organize, and share their game recommendations while bridging the gap in current mobile solutions?

Goals

The goal of this project was to develop a user-centered video game social cataloguing mobile app that not only simplifies the process of discovering and cataloguing games but also fosters a sense of community among gamers and empowers users to curate their adventures effortlessly.

Tools

  • Figma
  • Google Suite
  • Freeform

Understanding

User Research

Identifying current pain points and what users want and need

To gain a baseline understanding of user opinions I conducted 3 semi-structured interviews interviews were conducted with users who have played or bought video games in the last 6 months. They ranged in age, gender, and play styles from competitive to causal. With a set of guided questions, I spoke with each user for about 30 minutes and asked each user about their experience finding new games, organizing them, and what they most valued about the game launcher platforms they used. This helped me picture how user pain points and needs could be fulfilled by future designs.

  • I dont know what to look for.
  • I find most new games through content creators, rarely through shopping for them.
  • How do I know what I am interested in? Or know something exists?
  • It would be great to be able to follow youtubers and streamers to discover new things by their reviews.
  • Reviews dont really impact my decision but its nice to see.
  • I can't really organize my games beyond what I have now.
  • I trust people more than the algorithm.

User Feedback

Synthesized perceptions and perspectives

Users overwhelmingly favor game recommendations from real people rather than relying on algorithms or data-driven suggestions. They value authentic, human-driven insights over automated suggestions, seeking genuine connections within the gaming community

While most users resort to game launchers for purchasing and running games, they express frustration at finding the experience both overwhelming and underwhelming. Despite being a familiar platform, it fails to offer satisfying new game discoveries, leaving users searching for a more engaging and personalized experience.

Existing platforms suffer from cluttered filter and tagging systems, hindering confident navigation. Users struggle to explore content due to the overwhelming design, making it difficult to find games that match their preferences. Simplifying these systems is crucial to enhancing user confidence and encouraging seamless exploration.

Competitive Analysis

Market understanding and product evalution

Several other apps and websites within the mainstream gaming community were compared and evaluated on their visuals, offerings, and limitations. Strengths and weaknesses were identified as well gaps in the market.

Design Process

Sketching

Sketches, flows, and ideas

I ideated and drafted paper wireframes, drawing out potential solutions and user flows. The designs focused on laying out important elements such as navigation and search. I aimed to prioritize dynamic game information through motion actions and cards, ensuring a visually clean design that is both intuitive and interactive.

Wireframes

Ideating and early stages of design

I digitally recreated the paper wireframes into digital wireframes in Figma. The designs underwent slight refinement in the low fidelity prototyping phase by placing icons and adding interactive elements.

Usability Testing

Study Details

One round of unmoderated usability testing was conducted to improve the usability of the low fidelity wireframes. The 5 participants were allowed to take their time to respond to and complete a list of tasks using the prototype along with a survey at the end. Most took 15 minutes and gave good to highly positive answers on the survey. This study focused on answering the following questions:

  • Does the user have enough information?

  • Is there any pain points in need of improvement in the flow?

  • Is there any speciality information concerning the games, missing?

Study Results

An affinity map was created to organize and synthesize test responses and the following insights were used to improve the low fidelity prototypes before moving on to the high fidelity designs:

  • Users wanted more obvious access to lists of recommended users and the reason for suggestion.

  • Users found the search page pointless and redundant.

  • Users found text around adding a game to a list of games unclear.

Visual Design

Colors and Fonts

Brand identity and visual language

I aimed for a visual design that exudes neutrality, playfulness, and authenticity. Departing from the typical gaming aesthetics like neon, vaporwave, and black and white, I embraced a color palette inspired by the popular dark mode, using a range of serene blues complemented by a warm dark orange. This intentional combination creates a tranquil and sincere environment, punctured by welcoming and playful details.

Typography was also careful considered with the app focusing being visually and data dense. I opted for a clean and versatile sans-serif typefaces which ensures readability, especially across various devices and screen sizes, enhancing the overall accessibility of the app. This pairs well with another more geometric yet contemporary san sans, adds a touch of modernity to the typography, aligning with the app's aesthetic. This choice not only prioritizes inclusivity but also imbues the app with a sleek, contemporary vibe, ensuring that users engage with a visually appealing and user-friendly interface.

Design System

Designing for consistent look and feel

I created a streamlined design system, in which every element harmonizes to create an intuitive experience. Primary and secondary buttons offer clear calls to action, while tabs, inputs, checkboxes, and radio buttons ensure seamless interaction. Through meticulous attention to detail, GameShelf's consistent and user-friendly interface empowers gamers to effortlessly navigate the app, enhancing their gaming journey.

Final Designs

Main Pages

Easy to access recommendations and social activity

It was important to have the main pages connect the user to the main functions of the app with the navigation functionally recognizable. The central pages let the users explore social interactions, recommendations of games, lists, and profiles, and organize their games

Filtering and Sorting

A detailed and intensive filter and sort

Users stressed the importance of being able to search and filter games for by their individual wants. This included age ratings, and a filter for violent and gory games to be removed from the listings.

Game Details

Games brought up close and personal

Users want to be able to gleam everything they need from platform or OS, language support, genres, subgenres, and the reviews of others. Users enjoyed having two options for rating a game, one with just a star rating and another with stars and commenting

Genres and Themes

Browsing the categories with ease

Games can be categorized in many ways and in many systems. I based the organization data structure from the IGDB.com. I queried the data through a javascript script and used that data to visualize the categories.

Personal Lists

Creating lists for organizing games and sharing

Organizing games regardless of the platform or OS was of high importance to the users, this supported users to be able to watch certain games for more platform support or to categorize games by aesthetics or mechanics.

User Profiles

An easy to access hub for editing profile and following for updates

A important aspect of this project was to include ways for the lists and profiles on the app be connected to social media, with the shared links to be included in posts and link trees in profiles

Ratings and Checkpoints

Progress and letting others know what you thought

The private checkpoints allow for users to updated their 'Playing' games with progress.Users have two choices for saving progress, Checkpoint or Percentage, the two were chosen because there is not always clear 'checkpoints' in a game but it may have a percentage completed calculated. During testing users enjoyed having two options for rating a game, one with just a star rating and another with stars and a text review.

Challenges and Constraints

Designing a video game social cataloging mobile app poses several challenges. One of the primary challenges was to create an intuitive and engaging user experience that caters to a diverse audience of gamers and game buyers. Understanding the varying preferences, behaviors, and needs of this user base proved to be a complex task. Additionally, integrating social features seamlessly within the app, such as user reviews, ratings, and game recommendations, required thoughtful design solutions. Video game players with a game library from multiple platforms who want to organize all their games in one place.

  • A main challenge I encountered was how to make the app as democratic as possible without excluding anyone. The users I interviewed were aware of how color and aesthetics influenced them away from certain products.

  • Video games are an expansive category of storytelling with many subcategories attached to the visuals, mechanics, and development of them. I was faced with the challenge of creating a information architecture to support the data set of what a game is. Ideally more time would have been spent studying and developing a system for identifying games and their data to refine and clarify tags, subcategories, and create understanding of what makes a true roguelike game versus a games that has a rolling mechanic as well as a fire checkpoint system. This would have been crucial if working in tandem with a engineering team.

  • An encountered constraint was that had to build and scaffold my designs based on data from an API and at certain endpoints the data retrieved was incomplete or failed.

Continuous Improvement and Next Steps

Final Thoughts

This project posed the question - How to create an phone application that enhances video game discovery in a enjoyable, intuitive, and inclusive way? It challenged me to translate beautiful and efficient visual language that could be used cross platform and combining the strong information architecture of gaming language to make something users connected over and found new adventures to play. The project helped me learn more about running usability studies, about testing a prototype that has the basics without boiling the ocean to really understand user needs.

Next Steps

I would love to nurture and grow new ways for the app to improve and better serve its enthusiastic user base. Here are a few features and steps that I think could propel this project and make it even more enjoyable to use:

  • Work with a team or another individual and review the design to improve certain parts of the design.

  • Incorporate a onboarding experience with a quiz to help users set a base for future recommended games with the ability to change preferences in their profiles settings.

  • A informational dashboard in the profile for users to view data based on what they have played and reviewed.

  • Building out the desktop experience with a focus on including a OAuth game library import from other companies such as Steam, Nintendo, Mircosoft.

  • Conduct another user testing to identify pain points and user needs.
